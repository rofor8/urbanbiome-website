Rain Garden

Shallow, planted depression designed to receive, treat, and infiltrate/attenuate rainwater runoff from nearby impervious surfaces (e.g., roofs, driveways, patios). A type of bioretention system. (Source: NE GIDG 4.7, CIRIA C753 Ch 18, GI Design Guide).

Key Benefits (Derived from C753 Ch 18 & GI Guide)

  • Water Quantity: High Runoff Volume Reduction (Interception via soil storage/infiltration/evapotranspiration); Peak Flow Attenuation (surface ponding/filter media storage). (C753 S18.4)
  • Water Quality: Effective pollutant removal (TSS, Metals, Nutrients, Hydrocarbons) via filtration, adsorption, biological uptake. (C753 S18.5, Table 18.1)
  • Amenity: Attractive landscape feature; Educational opportunities. (C753 S18.6)
  • Biodiversity: Creates habitat for pollinators/invertebrates; Supports diverse planting; Can form part of habitat networks. (C753 S18.7; GI Guide)
  • Environmental: Groundwater recharge (if infiltrating); Reduces load on conventional drainage.

Technical Guidance (UK Context - aligned with C753 Ch 18 & GI Guide)

Rain gardens are small-scale bioretention systems, often used at plot level. Design principles follow C753 Ch 18 (Bioretention). Also see RHS/Wildfowl & Wetlands Trust guidance.

  • Siting (C753 S18.3): Suitable for draining small impermeable areas (roofs, patios, driveways - typically <500mÂ² per feature). Locate >5m from building foundations unless impermeable liner used or risk assessment confirms suitability (C753 S18.3). Avoid steep slopes unless terraced. Consider sunlight/shade for planting. Check for underground utilities.
  • Size & Depth (C753 S18.4): Surface area typically 2-10% of drained impermeable area. Shallow depression (150-300mm ponding depth typical). Total excavation depth depends on layers (filter media, drainage layer) - typically 0.6-1.2m.
  • Infiltration vs Underdrain (C753 S18.1): Design based on underlying soil infiltration rate (Ch 25). If infiltration >10mm/hr and groundwater >1m below base, can be unlined (allows infiltration). If infiltration poor or groundwater protection needed (Ch 26.3), use impermeable liner and perforated pipe underdrain connected to downstream drainage/soakaway.
  • Layers (C753 S18.1, S18.9):
    • Surface: Mulch (optional - avoid organic if risk of floating, gravel possible) or dense groundcover planting.
    • Filter Medium: Specified sandy loam mix (min 400mm, typically 500-800mm), meeting permeability (100-300mm/hr typ.) and horticultural requirements (C753 Box 18.1). Avoid standard topsoil.
    • Transition Layer: Graded sand/grit (min 100mm) OR filter geotextile to prevent fines washing into drainage layer (C753 S18.9.3).
    • Drainage Layer: Clean gravel/aggregate (min 100mm below pipe) or geocellular crates for storage/conveyance to underdrain (C753 S18.9.4).
  • Inlet (C753 S18.8.1): Direct downpipe connection (with splash pad/stone) or channel/rill from paved area. Use level spreader or energy dissipation (stones) to prevent erosion and promote even distribution. Consider pre-treatment (leaf guard, silt trap) for runoff from paved areas.
  • Outlet/Overflow (C753 S18.8.2): Essential for managing excess water. Can be raised outlet pipe connecting to underdrain, or surface overflow (weir/channel) to safe location. Must prevent blockage and erosion. Design for system to drain within 24-48hrs.
  • Planting (C753 S18.10; GI Guide): Select species tolerant of periodic inundation and subsequent drying. Use mix of grasses, perennials, small shrubs. UK Natives preferable for biodiversity (e.g., Yellow Flag Iris, Purple Loosestrife, Meadowsweet, Sedges). Ensure dense planting for surface protection and treatment.
  • Construction (C753 S18.11): Avoid soil compaction during excavation. Protect filter media from contamination. Ensure correct layer depths and levels. Establish planting quickly.
  • Maintenance (C753 S18.12): Regular weeding (esp. during establishment), check inlets/outlets for blockage, remove excess sediment/litter, prune/replace plants as needed, occasional mulch top-up (if used). Monitor drainage time.

Products Needed (Aligned with C753 Ch 18)

  • Filter Medium / Rain Garden Soil Mix
  • Transition Layer Sand/Grit
  • Drainage Layer Gravel/Aggregate
  • Filter Geotextile (if used instead of transition layer)
  • Impermeable Liner (if required)
  • Perforated Underdrain Pipe
  • Plants (Water-tolerant perennials, grasses, shrubs)
  • Mulch (Gravel/Pebbles - Optional)
  • Inlet Components (Splash stones, channel sections)
  • Outlet/Overflow Pipework & Headwall/Structure
