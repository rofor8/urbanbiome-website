Wildlife Pond

Creating pond habitats specifically designed to support local aquatic and amphibious wildlife, potentially forming part of a SuDS treatment train. (Source: NE GIDG, LNRS, CIRIA C753 Ch 23, GI Design Guide).

Key Benefits (Derived from C753 Ch 23 & GI Guide)

  • Biodiversity: High value habitat creation for aquatic/marginal plants, invertebrates (dragonflies, water beetles), amphibians (frogs, newts), potentially bathing/drinking water for birds/mammals. (C753 S23.7; GI Guide)
  • Water Management: Can provide attenuation storage; Water quality improvement (sedimentation, biological uptake); Interception (evaporation). (C753 S23.4, S23.5)
  • Amenity & Education: Attractive landscape feature; Educational resource (pond dipping); Enhances well-being. (C753 S23.6)
  • Environmental: Contributes to urban cooling; Can form part of ecological networks. (GI Guide)

Technical Guidance (UK Context - aligned with C753 Ch 23 & GI Guide)

Wildlife ponds prioritise ecological value. Design differs from purely ornamental ponds. Integrate with overall site SuDS and GI strategy. Refer to C753 Ch 23 for general pond design principles.

  • Siting (C753 S23.3): Locate in sunny, sheltered spot away from overhanging deciduous trees (reduces leaf litter). Consider proximity to other habitats for connectivity. Check soil type (need for liner) and groundwater levels (avoid permanent high levels unless designed for). Ensure safe distance from buildings.
  • Size & Shape: Variable, even small ponds valuable. Irregular shape maximises edge habitat. Max L:W ratio 5:1 recommended for SuDS function (C753 S23.2).
  • Depth Profile (C753 S23.2): CRITICAL for wildlife. Include varied depths: shallow margins/shelves (0-300mm) essential for emergent plants and amphibian access; deeper areas (600mm - 1m+) provide refuge from predators/frost. Avoid uniform deep excavation.
  • Edges & Slopes (C753 S23.2, S23.8): Gently sloping margins (<1:5) vital for wildlife access/egress and marginal planting. Include shallow beach area (pebbles/sand). Safety bench (min 1.5m wide, <1:15 slope) above water level important for safety and access (C753 S23.2).
  • Lining (C753 S23.9): Required if native soil too permeable. Options: flexible liner (butyl, EPDM, HDPE - requires protective underlay/overlay), preformed liner (limited shapes/sizes), puddled clay (traditional, requires skill). Ensure liner extends sufficiently above max water level and is well disguised at edges.
  • Water Source & Quality: Ideally fed by clean rainwater runoff (e.g., from roofs via RWH overflow, or treated runoff via upstream SuDS like swales/bioretention). Avoid direct input of heavily polluted runoff. Mains water top-up may be needed in drought (use dechlorinator if wildlife sensitive).
  • Planting (C753 S23.10; GI Guide): KEY for wildlife value. Use UK NATIVE species appropriate for different zones: Oxygenators (e.g., Hornwort); Floating (e.g., Frogbit); Shallow Marginals (e.g., Yellow Flag Iris, Water Mint, Brooklime); Damp Bankside (e.g., Meadowsweet, Purple Loosestrife). Plant sparsely initially, allow natural colonisation. Avoid non-native invasive species (check legislation).
  • Habitat Features (GI Guide): Include log piles, rock piles, or dense planting near pond edge for shelter/hibernation (amphibians, invertebrates).
  • Inlets/Outlets/Overflows (C753 S23.8): Design to prevent erosion and allow wildlife passage where appropriate (e.g., low flow pipe, vegetated spillway). Ensure safe overflow route for extreme events. Screen outlets appropriately if needed, avoiding designs that trap wildlife.
  • Maintenance (C753 S23.12): Relatively low if designed well. Periodic removal of excess decaying vegetation/silt (do in sections, leave material on bank temporarily for wildlife escape). Control invasive plant species. Occasional topping up in drought. Minimal intervention often best for wildlife. Avoid introducing fish.

Products Needed (Aligned with C753 Ch 23 & GI Guide)

  • Flexible Pond Liner (Butyl, EPDM, HDPE) & Underlay
  • Preformed Pond Liner (Various sizes)
  • Puddled Clay (Specialist supply)
  • Native Aquatic & Marginal Plants (Oxygenators, Floating, Emergent)
  • Aquatic Planting Soil/Baskets
  • Native Wildflower Seed Mix (Pond Edge/Damp Meadow)
  • Logs / Rocks (for habitat features)
  • Cobbles / Pebbles (for beach area)
  • Inlet/Outlet Pipework & Fittings
  • Overflow Spillway Components
  • Water Butt & Rain Diverter (as clean water source)
