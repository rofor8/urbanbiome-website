Blue Roof

Roof designed specifically to temporarily store and attenuate rainwater, releasing it slowly. Often combined with green roofs (blue-green roofs) or PV arrays. (Source: NE GIDG 4.5, CIRIA C753 S12.1).

Key Benefits (Derived from C753 principles)

  • Water Quantity: Significant Peak Flow Attenuation; Runoff Volume Reduction (via evaporation, potential reuse). (C753 S12.1)
  • Environmental: Reduces strain on downstream drainage infrastructure; Potential for rainwater reuse (irrigation, cooling).
  • Building Performance: Potential evaporative cooling effect.
  • (Amenity/Biodiversity benefits are low unless combined with green roof elements).

Technical Guidance (UK Context - aligned with C753 principles)

Blue roofs intentionally detain rainfall on a roof surface, typically using flow control outlets. Design requires careful structural and waterproofing considerations.

  • Structural Assessment (C753 S12.2): CRITICAL. Structure must support dead load of system components PLUS the maximum temporary water load (design storm depth) plus other loads (snow, maintenance). Load can be significant (>100 kg/mÂ² per 100mm water depth).
  • Waterproofing (C753 S12.8): High-performance, robust, fully sealed waterproofing membrane essential. Meticulous detailing at upstands, penetrations, and outlets critical. Integrity testing (flood test) highly recommended.
  • Storage Method: Water can be stored openly on the surface (requires level surface/containment), within a lightweight aggregate/substrate layer, or within proprietary modular storage units (similar to geocellular tanks - C753 Ch 21). Often combined with ballast, paving, or extensive/intensive green roof build-up above (blue-green roof).
  • Hydraulic Design (C753 S12.4, Ch 24): Calculate required storage volume based on contributing roof area, design storm (e.g., 1:100yr + climate change), and target discharge rate (often very low, e.g., greenfield rate or less).
  • Flow Control Outlet (C753 Ch 28): Key component. Typically a specialised roof drain outlet with integrated flow restrictor (e.g., orifice, vortex control) designed to release stored water at the target rate. Must be accessible for inspection/maintenance.
  • Overflow (C753 S12.8.8, Ch 28): Essential safety feature to handle rainfall exceeding design capacity or outlet blockage. Must safely convey excess water off the roof without causing damage.
  • Filtration: Requires filtration at inlet/outlet to prevent debris blocking flow restrictor.
  • Maintenance (C753 S12.12 principles): Regular inspection and cleaning of outlets, overflow, and filter components is essential to ensure correct function and prevent unintended water retention. Frequency depends on local conditions (debris load).

Products Needed (Aligned with C753 principles)

  • High-Performance Waterproofing Membrane
  • Blue Roof Flow Control Outlet Drain
  • Overflow Drain Components
  • Storage Layer/Modules (Optional - e.g., aggregate, crates)
  • Filter Geotextile/Components
  • Protection Fleece (Often required)
  • (May include ballast, paving, or green roof components if combined system)
