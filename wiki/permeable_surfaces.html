Permeable Surfaces

Replacing hard-standing surfaces with permeable/green surfaces (e.g., gravel, permeable paving, grass) to reduce surface water flooding. (Sources: LNRS, NE GIDG 4.4, CIRIA C753 Ch 20). Also known as Pervious Pavements.

Key Benefits (Derived from C753 Ch 20 & GI Guide)

  • Water Quantity: High Runoff Volume Reduction (Interception); Infiltration (if unlined); Peak Flow Attenuation (via sub-base storage). (C753 S20.5)
  • Water Quality: Good pollutant removal (TSS, Metals, Hydrocarbons) via filtration/adsorption/biodegradation in sub-base. (C753 S20.6)
  • Amenity: Can provide attractive hard surfacing; Reduces puddles/surface water; Potential for traffic calming; Can support tree planting (integrated pits). (C753 S20.7)
  • Environmental: Groundwater recharge (if infiltrating); Reduced thermal pollution of runoff.

Technical Guidance (UK Context - aligned with C753 Ch 20)

Pervious pavements provide structural surfaces for traffic/pedestrians while allowing rainwater infiltration into a storage sub-base. Refer to BS 7533-13:2009 for permeable paving design guidance.

  • Types (C753 S20.1): Porous (water through surface material - porous asphalt/concrete, resin-bound gravel, grass reinforcement) or Permeable (water through joints/voids - concrete blocks (CBPP), clay pavers, stone setts). Macro-pervious drains impermeable surface to permeable sub-base via channels.
  • System Configurations (C753 S20.1.9): Type A (Total Infiltration - no outfall); Type B (Partial Infiltration - overflow outfall); Type C (No Infiltration - lined with controlled outfall). Selection based on ground infiltration capacity (Ch 25), groundwater level (Ch 8.3), contamination risk (Ch 8.2).
  • Site Assessment (C753 S20.3): Check soil infiltration rates (for Type A/B), subgrade strength (CBR - S20.9.3), groundwater levels, contamination, slope, location of utilities.
  • Structural Design (C753 S20.9): Pavement structure (surface, bedding, sub-base, capping) must support design traffic loads (categories in C753 Table 20.5). Design depth based on structural needs OR hydraulic storage needs, whichever is greater. Use appropriate methods (e.g., BS 7533-13, analytical design).
  • Hydraulic Design (C753 S20.5): Ensure surface infiltration rate adequate (min 2500 mm/hr new, 250 mm/hr long-term - S20.5.1). Calculate required sub-base storage volume based on design storm (e.g. 1:100yr + climate change), contributing area (including run-on, max 2:1 impermeable:pervious ratio for surface flow), infiltration rate (if Type A/B), and controlled discharge rate (if Type B/C). Consider check dams on slopes >5% (S20.5.1).
  • Water Quality Design (C753 S20.6): Achieved via filtration/treatment within pavement layers (bedding, sub-base, geotextiles). Geotextile selection important (filtration vs separation - Ch 30.5). Protect sub-base from excessive fines during construction and operation. Pre-treatment needed for runoff sources with high sediment/pollutant loads.
  • Materials (C753 S20.11): Specify appropriate materials: Surface (e.g., CBPP blocks, porous asphalt mix); Bedding/Jointing grit (angular, free-draining, e.g., 2-6.3mm); Sub-base (open-graded crushed aggregate, e.g., Type 3 or 4/20, 4/40 to BS 7533-13 - must be permeable with >30% voids); Geotextiles (separation/filtration/protection); Liners (impermeable geomembrane for Type C).
  • Construction (C753 S20.13): CRITICAL for performance. Avoid subgrade compaction. Protect sub-base/surface from contamination during construction (mud, debris). Ensure correct aggregate compaction (avoid crushing). Use appropriate laying techniques for surface type.
  • Maintenance (C753 S20.14): Regular (annual typical) surface cleaning essential to maintain infiltration (vacuum sweeping recommended). Check outlets/overflows. Weed control may be needed. Periodic deep cleaning/joint refurbishment may be needed long-term.

Products Needed (Aligned with C753 Ch 20)

  • Permeable Paving Units (CBPP, Clay, Stone)
  • Porous Asphalt / Concrete Mix
  • Resin Bound Aggregate System
  • Grass Reinforcement Grids (Plastic/Concrete)
  • Jointing / Bedding Aggregate (e.g., 2-6.3mm clean angular grit)
  • Permeable Sub-base Aggregate (e.g., Type 3, 4/20, 4/40)
  • Geotextiles (Separation/Filtration/Protection)
  • Impermeable Geomembrane Liner (for Type C systems)
  • Perforated Pipes / Fin Drains (for outlets if needed)
  • Flow Control Devices / Chambers (if needed)
