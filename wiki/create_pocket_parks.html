Create Pocket Parks

Creating small pocket parks/green spaces in unused or underused urban spaces, prioritising areas of green space deficiency. (Grant: CGS) (Sources: LNRS, NE GIDG 4.16, 6.4, GI Design Guide).

Key Benefits (Derived from GI Guide principles & C753 Ch 5/6)

  • Social & Amenity: Provides local green space for relaxation, play, social interaction; Improves neighbourhood aesthetics & sense of place; Particularly valuable in areas lacking larger parks.
  • Environmental: Contributes to urban cooling; Improves local air quality; Potential for rainwater infiltration/management via integrated SuDS features.
  • Biodiversity: Creates small habitat patches; Can act as stepping stones in ecological networks; Supports pollinators/invertebrates if appropriately planted.
  • Health & Well-being: Access to nature improves mental and physical health.

Technical Guidance (UK Context - aligned with GI Guide & C753 principles)

Pocket parks transform small urban spaces into valuable community assets. Design should maximise multi-functionality within limited space.

  • Site Identification & Suitability: Identify potential underused spaces (small vacant plots, wide verges, leftover spaces). Assess ownership, accessibility, sunlight, existing features, surrounding context, community need/support.
  • Design Concept: Define primary purpose (e.g., quiet seating, play, food growing, biodiversity focus). Design layout creatively to maximise use of small space. Consider boundaries, entrances, pathways, seating, planting areas, potential SuDS features. Engage community in design process (C753 Ch 34).
  • Planting Design (GI Guide - Planting; C753 Ch 29): Choose robust, low-maintenance species suitable for urban conditions and site aspect. Use mix of trees (small varieties), shrubs, perennials, grasses for structure, seasonal interest, and biodiversity value (native species where possible). Consider vertical greening (climbers on walls/fences) to maximise greenery.
  • SuDS Integration (C753): Incorporate small-scale SuDS where feasible: Rain gardens/bioretention planters (Ch 18) taking runoff from adjacent paths/roofs; Permeable paving for paths/seating areas (Ch 20); Tree pits designed to accept runoff (Ch 19).
  • Hard Landscaping: Use durable, attractive materials for paths, seating, edging. Consider recycled/sustainable materials. Ensure accessible design.
  • Soil Management (GI Guide - Soils; C753 Ch 29.5): Urban soils often poor/compacted. Assess existing soil, remediate/improve as necessary. Import good quality topsoil (BS 3882) for planting beds.
  • Community Involvement & Management: Engage local community/potential users in design, creation, and long-term management. Establish clear maintenance responsibilities (local authority, community group).
  • Maintenance (GI Guide - Maintenance; C753 Ch 32): Requires regular basic maintenance (litter picking, weeding, watering during establishment) and periodic tasks (pruning, SuDS feature checks). Management plan essential.

Products Needed (Aligned with GI Guide principles - variable)

  • Trees (Small/Medium varieties suitable for urban spaces)
  • Shrubs, Perennials, Grasses (Robust varieties)
  • Topsoil / Soil Improver
  • Permeable Paving Materials (Optional)
  • Path Edging Materials
  • Benches / Litter Bins
  • Planters / Raised Beds (Optional)
  • Climbing Plants & Support Structures (Optional)
  • (Potentially SuDS components - see Rain Garden, Permeable Surfaces, SuDS Tree Pit pages)
