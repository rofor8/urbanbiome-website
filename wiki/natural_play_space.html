Natural Play Space

Play areas designed with natural features like logs, boulders, trees, sand, water, and diverse planting instead of predominantly manufactured equipment. (Source: NE GIDG 4.20, GI Design Guide - Play).

Key Benefits (Derived from GI Guide principles)

  • Social & Health: Encourages imaginative, creative, and physical play; Develops risk awareness & management skills; Improves physical health & motor skills; Provides sensory experiences; Positive impact on mental well-being.
  • Environmental: Can incorporate SuDS features (e.g., playful swales, rain gardens with channels, seasonal water play); Contributes to urban cooling; Uses natural/sustainable materials.
  • Biodiversity: Creates habitat opportunities within play features (log piles, planting, ponds); Connects children with nature.
  • Amenity & Aesthetics: Creates unique, engaging, and often more aesthetically pleasing play environments compared to standard playgrounds.

Technical Guidance (UK Context - aligned with GI Guide principles)

Natural play focuses on using landscape elements and natural materials to create stimulating play opportunities. Design must balance creative play with safety standards. Refer to guidance from Play England, Learning Through Landscapes.

  • Design Principles: Aim for variety in texture, levels, and features. Incorporate elements for climbing (boulders, logs, mounds), balancing (logs, stepping stones), hiding (dens, planting), sensory experiences (sand, water, textured plants), and loose parts play (sticks, stones - requires management).
  • Safety Standards: Natural play spaces must still comply with relevant play safety standards (e.g., BS EN 1176). Requires careful risk-benefit assessment. Design features to minimise unacceptable risks (e.g., ensuring safe fall heights/surfacing under climbing structures, managing water depth/access).
  • Materials: Use natural materials like timber (durable, untreated hardwoods or treated softwood suitable for ground contact), boulders, sand, gravel, woodchip. Source sustainably where possible.
  • Earthworks & Topography: Create mounds, banks, hollows, channels for physical play and to help define spaces. Ensure slope stability.
  • Planting: Integrate robust planting for play value (hiding, den-making - e.g., willow structures, dense shrubs), sensory experience (textured/scented plants), and habitat. Avoid toxic plants or those with sharp thorns in high-contact areas. Use trees for shade and climbing opportunities (select appropriate species/form, protect trunk base).
  • Water Play: Can include pumps, channels, shallow pools, sand/water mixing areas. Requires careful design regarding water source (mains often needed for hygiene), drainage, water depth, supervision needs, and safety surfacing. Can potentially integrate with rain gardens or treated SuDS outflow.
  • Surfacing: Use appropriate impact-absorbing surfaces under equipment where required by safety standards (e.g., woodchip, sand, specialist rubber). Elsewhere use grass, sand, gravel, bare earth, timber decking.
  • Maintenance: Requires specific maintenance regime. Regular checks for safety (splinters, loose parts, fall surfacing depth). Pruning/management of planting. Replenishing loose materials (sand, woodchip). Checking water play features.

Products Needed (Aligned with GI Guide principles - variable)

  • Play Grade Logs / Boulders
  • Play Sand / Bark / Woodchip
  • Timber for Play Structures / Dens / Decking
  • Plants (Robust Shrubs, Grasses, Sensory Plants)
  • Trees (Suitable for climbing/shade)
  • Water Pump / Play Channel Components (Optional)
  • Impact Absorbing Safety Surfacing
  • Landscape Fabric / Fixings
