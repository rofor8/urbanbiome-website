Biosolar Roof

Combination of an extensive green roof with photovoltaic (PV) solar panels, integrating renewable energy generation with green infrastructure benefits. (Sources: NE GIDG 4.5, IGNITION, GI Design Guide, C753 Ch 12 principles).

Key Benefits (Derived from C753, GI Guide & IGNITION)

  • Energy Generation: Produces renewable electricity on-site.
  • Enhanced PV Performance: Green roof substrate/vegetation provides evaporative cooling effect, which can slightly increase PV panel efficiency compared to conventional roof mounting (GI Guide).
  • Water Management: Runoff Volume Reduction & Peak Flow Attenuation from green roof element (C753 S12.4).
  • Building Performance: Thermal Insulation & increased roof lifespan from green roof element.
  • Environmental: Urban Cooling, Air Quality improvement, Carbon reduction (energy generation & sequestration).
  • Biodiversity: Green roof element provides habitat (C753 S12.7).

Technical Guidance (UK Context - aligned with C753 Ch 12 & GI Guide)

Biosolar roofs combine PV arrays with extensive green roof systems. Design requires careful integration of both technologies.

  • System Design: Typically involves mounting PV panels on frames above an extensive green roof substrate/vegetation layer. Requires specialist design to ensure compatibility.
  • Structural Assessment (C753 S12.2): CRITICAL. Roof structure must support combined dead load (green roof system, PV panels, mounting system) plus live loads (water saturation, snow, wind uplift on panels, maintenance access). Loads are higher than standard extensive green roofs.
  • Green Roof Build-up (C753 Ch 12): Follows principles for extensive green roofs (waterproofing, root barrier, drainage, filter, substrate, vegetation). Substrate depth typically 80-150mm.
  • PV System & Mounting: Panels mounted on framework designed not to penetrate waterproofing. Framework often ballasted by the green roof substrate itself, or uses specialised low-profile mounts. Ensure sufficient height above vegetation to avoid shading of panels (consider mature plant height). Consider panel layout, orientation, tilt for optimal energy generation, and safe maintenance access routes around panels.
  • Vegetation Selection (C753 S12.10): Use low-growing, drought-tolerant extensive green roof species (e.g., sedums, low herbs/grasses) that won't overgrow/shade panels. Consider shade-tolerant species for areas beneath panels.
  • Wind Load & Ballast: Requires specific wind load calculations considering combined system (panels increase uplift risk). Substrate weight acts as ballast for PV mounting system; ensure sufficient depth/weight according to calculations.
  • Edge Detail & Fire Breaks (C753 S12.8.1): Vegetation-free gravel/paved margins required around perimeter, penetrations, and potentially around PV arrays according to fire regulations (GRO Code/Building Regs Part B).
  • Electrical Installation: PV electrical installation (cabling, inverters) must be carried out by qualified professionals to relevant standards (e.g., MCS). Cable routing needs careful integration with green roof layers.
  • Maintenance (C753 S12.12): Requires combined maintenance strategy. Green roof maintenance (weeding, outlet checks) plus PV maintenance (panel cleaning, electrical checks). Ensure safe access for both. Vegetation beneath panels may need specific management.

Products Needed (Aligned with C753 Ch 12 & PV standards)

  • Photovoltaic (PV) Panels
  • PV Mounting System (Biosolar compatible - ballasted/fixed)
  • PV Inverter, Cabling & Electrical Components
  • Extensive Green Roof Components (Waterproofing, Root Barrier, Drainage, Filter, Substrate, Vegetation - see Extensive Green Roof page)
  • Gravel/Pebbles (for margins/ballast)
