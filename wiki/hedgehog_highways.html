Hedgehog Highways

Providing ground-level connectivity for hedgehogs by creating small holes in garden fences, walls, or gravel boards, allowing them to move between gardens to forage and find mates. Also includes providing suitable habitat like log piles or wild areas. (Sources: LNRS, NE GIDG 4.9, 4.15, GI Design Guide - Connectivity).

Key Benefits (Derived from GI Guide principles)

  • Biodiversity: Improves habitat connectivity for hedgehogs (a species of conservation concern); Allows access to larger foraging areas; Reduces risk of road mortality by providing alternative routes.
  • Ecosystem Services: Hedgehogs contribute to garden pest control (slugs, snails, insects).
  • Community Engagement: Raises awareness of local wildlife; Simple action for residents to implement.

Technical Guidance (UK Context - aligned with GI Guide principles)

Hedgehogs roam large distances nightly. Fences and walls fragment their habitat. Simple gaps allow essential movement. Refer to guidance from People's Trust for Endangered Species (PTES) Hedgehog Street campaign, British Hedgehog Preservation Society (BHPS).

  • Creating Gaps: Cut a hole approx. 13cm x 13cm (CD case size) at the base of solid fences (timber panels, concrete gravel boards). For new fences, incorporate gaps from the start (e.g., use hedgehog-friendly gravel boards with pre-cut holes, raise panels slightly off ground where secure). Ensure hole is smooth-edged.
  • Location: Create gaps in multiple boundary fences within a neighbourhood to create linked-up habitat. Avoid creating gaps leading directly onto busy roads or hazardous areas.
  • Habitat Provision: Complement connectivity with suitable habitat within gardens/developments: Log piles, leaf piles, compost heaps (check before disturbing), areas of longer grass, dense shrubbery, wildlife ponds with escape ramps. Avoid using slug pellets and netting. Provide shallow dishes of water, especially in dry weather.
  • New Developments: Specify hedgehog highway gaps in fencing/boundary treatments as standard within planning conditions/design codes.
  • Community Action: Encourage neighbours to link their gardens by creating holes in shared fences.

Products Needed (Aligned with GI Guide principles)

  • Hedgehog Friendly Gravel Boards (with holes)
  • Hedgehog Highway Sign/Marker (Optional)
  • Hedgehog House/Hibernaculum (Optional habitat feature)
  • (Plus tools for cutting holes: Jigsaw, Drill)
  • (Habitat materials: Logs, Leaves - often available on site)
