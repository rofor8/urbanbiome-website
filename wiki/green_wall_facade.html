Green Façade

Climbing plants growing directly on or against a wall, potentially using a support structure. Lower maintenance than living walls. (Sources: IGNITION, NE GIDG 4.6)

Key Benefits

  • Energy Saving (Cooling): 15%
  • Indoor Temperature Reduction: 2.7°C
  • Air Temperature Reduction: 1-3°C
  • Exterior Wall Temperature Reduction: 3°C
  • Noise Reduction: 2.6 db
  • Property Value Uplift: 2.5% (Assumed similar to Living Wall)
  • (Air quality/water benefits likely less than living wall)

Technical Guidance (UK Context)

  • Wall Assessment: Check the structural integrity and condition of the wall surface. Ensure mortar joints are sound, especially if self-clinging climbers (like Ivy) are considered, as they can penetrate weak mortar over time. Check for existing damp issues.
  • Planting Method Selection:
    • Direct Planting (Self-Clingers): Plants like Ivy (Hedera helix), Virginia Creeper (Parthenocissus quinquefolia), or Climbing Hydrangea (Hydrangea petiolaris) attach directly to the wall. Requires minimal support structure but needs sound wall surface and careful management to prevent damage or excessive spread onto windows/roofs.
    • Support Structure (Twining/Scrambling Climbers): For plants like Honeysuckle (Lonicera periclymenum), Jasmine (Jasminum spp.), Clematis, Wisteria, or climbing Roses, a support structure is essential. This can be trellis (wood or metal), wire systems (horizontal or grid), or mesh panels fixed to the wall with appropriate stand-off brackets (creating a gap of 50-100mm+ is recommended for air circulation and maintenance).
  • Support Structure Installation: If used, ensure the trellis/wires/mesh are securely fixed to the wall using appropriate fixings for the wall type and capable of supporting the mature weight of the plant (including wind load). Use stand-off brackets to create an air gap.
  • Planting Location: Plant climbers in prepared soil at the base of the wall. Improve soil with compost if necessary. Ensure adequate drainage. If planting in containers, use large, deep containers suitable for the chosen climber's mature size.
  • Plant Selection: Choose climbers appropriate for the aspect (sun/shade), wall height, UK climate, and desired effect (evergreen/deciduous, flowering). Consider native options like Ivy or Honeysuckle for biodiversity. Be mindful of the vigour of species like Wisteria or Russian Vine (Fallopia baldschuanica), which require strong support and regular pruning.
  • Training & Pruning: Initially, tie in young shoots to guide them onto the support structure. Regular pruning is required to keep plants within their allotted space, remove dead/damaged growth, encourage flowering (for relevant species), and prevent them from blocking gutters, windows, or vents. Pruning requirements vary greatly by species.
  • Watering & Feeding: Water regularly during establishment and dry periods, especially for container-grown plants. Feed annually in spring with a general-purpose fertiliser or as required by the specific plant.
  • Maintenance: Generally lower maintenance than living walls. Primarily involves annual pruning, training, checking support structures/fixings, and managing pests/diseases if they occur.

Products Needed

  • Climbing Plants (Self-clinging or Twining/Scrambling)
  • Trellis Panels (Wood/Metal - Optional)
  • Wire Support System (Wires, vine eyes, tensioners - Optional)
  • Mesh Support Panels (Optional)
  • Stand-off Wall Brackets (Optional)
  • Wall Fixings (Appropriate for wall type)
  • Compost / Soil Improver
  • Plant Ties / Support Clips
  • Large Containers (If not planting in ground)
  • General Purpose Fertiliser
