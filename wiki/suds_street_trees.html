SuDS-Enabled Street Trees

Street trees combined with sustainable drainage features (e.g., larger pits, structural soils) to manage rainwater. (Sources: IGNITION, NE GIDG 4.10)

Technical Guidance (UK Context)

  • Site Assessment & Design: Includes all checks for standard street trees, PLUS detailed consideration of surface water flows, catchment area, infiltration rates, and existing drainage infrastructure. Design needs to ensure adequate water storage volume in the tree pit system (often significantly larger than standard pits), safe overflow routes for extreme rainfall events, and integration with surrounding pavement levels. Consult CIRIA SuDS Manual (C753) and local authority SuDS guidance. Planning permission and specific SuDS approval might be required.
  • Species Selection: As per standard street trees, but with added emphasis on waterlogging tolerance, especially if prolonged inundation is possible in the pit design. Species like Alder (Alnus glutinosa), Willow (Salix spp. - choose less vigorous varieties), Downy Birch (Betula pubescens), or specific cultivars of other trees known for wet tolerance might be suitable.
  • Pit Construction: Significantly more complex. Typically involves excavating a larger pit, lining it with geotextile membranes (permeable or impermeable depending on infiltration goals/contamination risk), installing engineered/structural soils (e.g., Amsterdam tree soil) or modular crate systems to provide load-bearing capacity under pavements while allowing root growth and water storage. Incorporate inlet pipes/channels for surface water entry and potentially an underdrain/overflow pipe connected to the main drainage system.
  • Planting Medium: Use specified engineered soils or structural soil mixes designed for load-bearing and drainage properties. Avoid standard topsoil which can compact easily under load.
  • Support & Protection: As per standard street trees, but ensure guards/grilles are compatible with the SuDS inlet/outlet design and allow for maintenance access.
  • Watering: While designed to capture rainwater, supplementary watering is still crucial during establishment, especially in dry spells, as the engineered soils may drain quickly initially.
  • Maintenance: Requires regular inspection of inlets/outlets for blockages (sediment, litter), checking overflow function, monitoring tree health, and managing vegetation within the pit (if any surface planting is included). More complex than standard tree maintenance.

Products Needed

  • Tree Sapling (SuDS Tolerant Species)
  • Structural Soil / Engineered Soil Mix
  • Geotextile Membrane (Permeable/Impermeable)
  • Modular Soakaway/Attenuation Crates (Optional)
  • Inlet Pipes / Surface Water Channels
  • Overflow / Underdrain Pipework & Fittings
  • Heavy Duty Tree Grille (SuDS Compatible)
  • Tree Stakes & Ties
  • Mulch (e.g., Aggregate/Stone - often preferred over bark in SuDS pits)
  • Watering Tube/Bag (Optional)
