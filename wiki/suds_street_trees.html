SuDS-Enabled Street Trees

Street trees combined with sustainable drainage features (e.g., larger pits, structural soils) to manage rainwater. (Sources: IGNITION, NE GIDG 4.10)

Key Benefits (Derived from C753, GI Guide & IGNITION)

  • Enhanced Rainwater Runoff Retained: 78% (C753 Ch 19.4); Significant Interception & Volume Control
  • Enhanced Stormwater Runoff Delay: 81% (C753 Ch 19.4); Peak Flow Attenuation
  • Water Quality Improvement: Filtration, pollutant uptake (C753 Ch 19.5)
  • Air Temperature Reduction: 3Â°C (C753 Ch 5)
  • Carbon Sequestered: 5.5 kg/tree/yr
  • Noise Reduction: 4 db/tree
  • Amenity & Well-being: Enhanced streetscape, potential biodiversity focus (GI Guide)

Technical Guidance (UK Context - aligned with C753 Ch 19, Ch 18 & GI Guide)

SuDS-enabled tree pits are specifically designed to receive, store, and treat surface water runoff, integrating water management with urban greening.

  • Site Assessment & Design (C753 Ch 7, S19.2): Includes all checks for standard street trees, PLUS detailed hydrological assessment: surface water flow paths, contributing catchment area (impermeable surfaces draining to pit), infiltration rates (if designed to infiltrate), potential contamination, and integration with existing/proposed drainage network (overflows). Requires careful engineering design (often using hydraulic modelling) to manage water levels, inflow/outflow rates, and safe overflow routes for extreme events (Ch 24.12). Pit design must balance water storage volume with uncompacted soil volume for healthy root growth (TDAG guidance). Consult CIRIA SuDS Manual (C753) and local authority SuDS guidance. Specific SuDS/planning approval likely required.
  • Species Selection (C753 S19.3; GI Guide - Planting): As per standard street trees, but with added emphasis on tolerance to periodic waterlogging/inundation and potentially higher pollutant loads depending on runoff source. Species like Alder (Alnus glutinosa), Downy Birch (Betula pubescens), selected Willows (Salix spp.), or specific cultivars may be more suitable. Consider pollutant tolerance.
  • Pit Construction & Components (C753 S19.8, S19.9, Ch 18): Significantly more complex than standard pits. Typically involves larger/deeper excavation. May require impermeable liner if infiltration unsuitable (C753 Ch 8.2) or permeable geotextiles for separation/filtration (Ch 30.5). Must incorporate load-bearing structure under pavements (structural soils - C753 S19.2.1, S19.9.2; or modular units/suspended pavement - C753 S19.2.2, S19.2.3). Requires designed inlets (e.g., kerb inlets with pre-treatment, permeable channels - C753 Ch 28.4) to direct surface water into the pit, often incorporating silt traps/pre-treatment. A controlled outlet/underdrain (C753 Ch 28.5) connected to the main drainage system or downstream SuDS component is essential. Engineered overflow mechanism required for capacity exceedance.
  • Planting & Filter Medium (C753 S19.9, Ch 18): Use specified engineered/structural soils designed for load-bearing, aeration, appropriate drainage/water retention, and pollutant treatment (potentially meeting bioretention filter media specs - C753 Box 18.1). Standard topsoil unsuitable under pavements. May include drainage/storage layer (e.g., lightweight aggregate, crates) below soil medium.
  • Support & Protection (C753 S19.11): As per standard street trees, but tree grilles/guards must be compatible with water inlets/outlets and allow maintenance access (C753 S19.8.4).
  • Watering (C753 S19.11): Supplemental watering is still crucial during establishment (first 2-3 years), as engineered soils may drain quickly or inflows may be insufficient during dry spells.
  • Maintenance (C753 S19.12, Ch 32): Requires regular inspection of inlets/outlets/overflows for blockages (sediment, litter - C753 S32.6). Monitor tree health and water levels. Manage surface vegetation/weeds. Check structural integrity of grilles/guards. Potential need for periodic flushing or removal of accumulated pollutants/sediment from pre-treatment zones or substrate surface. More complex and potentially costly than standard tree maintenance.

Products Needed (Aligned with C753 Ch 19 & SuDS components)

  • Tree Stock (SuDS Tolerant Species to BS 8545)
  • Structural Soil / Engineered Soil Mix
  • Geotextile Membrane (Permeable/Impermeable)
  • Modular Soakaway/Attenuation Crates (Optional)
  • Inlet Pipes / Surface Water Channels
  • Overflow / Underdrain Pipework & Fittings
  • Heavy Duty Tree Grille (SuDS Compatible)
  • Tree Stakes & Ties
  • Mulch (e.g., Aggregate/Stone - often preferred over bark in SuDS pits)
  • Watering Tube/Bag (Optional)
