Rainwater Harvesting (New Dev)

Collecting rainwater runoff (typically from roofs) for storage and non-potable reuse. (Source: LNRS, CIRIA C753 Ch 11, GI Design Guide). Note: ID from data refers to New Dev, but principles apply generally.

Key Benefits (Derived from C753 Ch 11 & GI Guide)

  • Water Resource Management: Reduces demand on mains water supply (water conservation); Provides water for non-potable uses (toilet flushing, laundry, garden irrigation). (C753 S11.1)
  • Water Quantity Management: Reduces runoff volume from connected surfaces (Interception); Can contribute to peak flow attenuation if designed with specific surface water management storage volume. (C753 S11.1, S11.3)
  • Economic: Potential reduction in water bills.
  • Environmental: Reduces energy used for water treatment/supply; Reduces pressure on drainage infrastructure.

Technical Guidance (UK Context - aligned with C753 Ch 11)

Rainwater Harvesting (RWH) systems collect, store, and distribute rainwater. Design must consider yield (supply) vs demand, water quality, and integration with building plumbing and site drainage. Refer to BS 8515:2009+A1:2013 for detailed code of practice.

  • System Types (C753 S11.1): Gravity-fed (storage elevated), Pumped (storage at ground/below ground, direct or via header tank), or Composite. Pumped systems are most common for whole-house supply.
  • Yield Calculation (C753 S11.3.2): Estimate yield based on collecting surface area (roof plan area), average annual rainfall (AAR), runoff coefficient (depends on roof type, see C753 Table 11.4/11.5), and filter efficiency (typ. 0.9).
  • Demand Calculation (C753 S11.3.2): Estimate non-potable demand based on building occupancy (domestic - see C753 Table 11.3) or building type (commercial - see BS 8525-2) and intended uses (e.g., toilet flushing ~20 l/person/day, washing machine ~20 l/person/day, garden irrigation variable).
  • Tank Sizing (C753 S11.3): Size depends on objective:
    • Water Conservation Only: Size based on balancing yield vs demand (e.g., BS 8515 suggests tank volume â‰ˆ 5% of annual yield or demand, whichever is smaller).
    • Surface Water Management (Passive Control): Requires additional storage volume above the 'supply' volume to capture a specific design storm depth (e.g., 5mm for Interception, or volume for 1:100yr event). Design calculations complex (see C753 Box 11.4, 11.5, 11.6), performance relies on demand being reliably greater than yield (YR/DN < 0.9-0.95 typical).
    • Surface Water Management (Active Control): Uses sensors/forecasts to manage water level, guaranteeing storage availability for storm events. More complex/costly but offers reliable performance regardless of YR/DN ratio (C753 S11.3.4).
  • Components & Water Quality (C753 S11.4, S11.7, S11.8): Requires leaf filter/gutter guard, first flush diverter (optional but recommended), calming inlet, storage tank (material choice affects water quality/durability - GRP, PE, concrete), pump (if needed), filtration (appropriate level for intended use), overflow (connected appropriately to drainage), and back-up mains water feed (must have correct air gap - Water Supply Regs). Treatment level depends on use (basic filtration often sufficient for garden/toilets, further treatment/disinfection needed for higher risk uses).
  • Installation (C753 S11.10): Ensure correct pipework (distinguish from potable water - WRAS guidelines), secure tank installation (consider buoyancy if below water table), correct electrical installation for pumps/controls.
  • Maintenance (C753 S11.11): Regular checks needed: clean filters/gutters (annually min.), inspect tank for sediment (annually), check pump/controls function. Periodic tank desludging may be required (frequency depends on filtration effectiveness).

Products Needed (Aligned with C753 Ch 11 & BS 8515)

  • Storage Tank (Below/Above Ground - GRP, PE, Concrete)
  • Pre-Tank Filter / Leaf Guard / First Flush Diverter
  • Calmed Inlet
  • Pump & Controller (Submersible/External, potentially with mains backup control)
  • Post-Tank Filtration Unit (Cartridge, UV etc. as needed)
  • Non-Potable Water Pipework & Fittings (Correctly labelled)
  • Mains Water Top-up System (with compliant air gap)
  • Overflow Outlet Components
