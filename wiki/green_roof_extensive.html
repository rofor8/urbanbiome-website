Extensive Green Roof

Thin layer of vegetation (<150mm substrate) on a roof, low maintenance. Often sedum or wildflowers. (Sources: IGNITION, NE GIDG 4.5)

Key Benefits

  • Energy Saving (Below): 6.7%
  • Noise Reduction: 11 db
  • Rainwater Runoff Retained: 62%
  • Stormwater Runoff Delay: 73%
  • Surface Temperature Reduction: 6.5°C
  • Air Temperature Reduction (Above): 0.8°C
  • Carbon Sequestered: 1.28 kg/m²/yr
  • Carbon Stored (Veg): 0.375 kg/m²
  • Air Pollutant Removal (NO2): 24% (Above roof)
  • Air Pollutant Removal (PM10): 14% (Above roof)
  • Water Quality (NO3 Reduction): 80%
  • Water Quality (TSS Reduction): 70%
  • Water Quality (P Reduction): 67%
  • Property Value Uplift (Non-Accessible): 2.9%

Technical Guidance (UK Context)

  • Structural Assessment: CRITICAL FIRST STEP. A qualified structural engineer MUST assess the existing roof structure's load-bearing capacity. Extensive green roofs add significant weight (saturated weight typically 60-150 kg/m²). The assessment must confirm the roof can safely support this additional load, including snow load considerations.
  • Waterproofing: Ensure the existing roof waterproofing membrane is in excellent condition, root-resistant (or protected by a separate root barrier), and properly detailed, especially around edges and penetrations. Address any defects BEFORE installing the green roof.
  • Roof Slope: Suitable for flat roofs or slopes typically up to 10-15 degrees. Steeper slopes require specific engineering solutions (e.g., shear barriers) to prevent slippage.
  • Drainage: A dedicated drainage layer is essential to allow excess water to escape, preventing waterlogging. This is often a cuspated (dimpled) plastic sheet or drainage board. Ensure roof outlets/gutters are clear and adequately sized, incorporating inspection chambers. Include a filter fleece/geotextile above the drainage layer to prevent substrate fines from clogging it.
  • Substrate: Use a lightweight, engineered green roof substrate specifically designed for extensive systems (typically 60-150mm deep). Do NOT use standard topsoil, which is too heavy and compacts easily. Substrate composition often includes crushed brick/tile, Leca (lightweight expanded clay aggregate), lava, pumice, and some organic matter.
  • Vegetation: Drought-tolerant, low-growing, low-maintenance plants are key. Sedum species are most common, often supplied as pre-grown mats or blankets for quick establishment. Alternatively, plug plants or seeds (including native UK wildflower mixes suitable for green roofs) can be used, but establishment takes longer.
  • Edge Detail & Fire Breaks: Install gravel margins or paving slabs around the perimeter, roof lights, and other penetrations (typically 300-500mm wide) to act as vegetation-free fire breaks and prevent vegetation from blocking drainage outlets. Comply with UK building regulations regarding fire safety (e.g., Approved Document B).
  • Installation: Install components sequentially: Root barrier (if needed), drainage layer, filter fleece, substrate, vegetation. Follow manufacturer guidelines precisely.
  • Maintenance: Although low-maintenance, some care is needed: Remove unwanted weeds (especially tree seedlings) 1-2 times per year. Check drainage outlets are clear. Apply slow-release fertiliser sparingly only if needed (often not required for sedum). Water only during prolonged drought periods, especially during establishment.

Products Needed

  • Root Barrier Membrane (If waterproofing isn't root-resistant)
  • Drainage Layer (e.g., Cuspated Sheet, Drainage Board)
  • Filter Fleece / Geotextile
  • Extensive Green Roof Substrate (Lightweight)
  • Vegetation (e.g., Sedum Mat/Blanket, Plug Plants, Seeds)
  • Edge Retainment Profile/System
  • Gravel/Pebbles (for vegetation-free margins)
  • Drainage Outlets / Inspection Chambers
