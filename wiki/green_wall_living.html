Living Wall

Vegetation growing on a vertical module/system attached to a wall, often with integrated irrigation. (Sources: IGNITION, NE GIDG 4.6)

Key Benefits

  • Energy Saving (Adjacent): 8%
  • Indoor Temperature Reduction: 4.8°C
  • Air Temperature Reduction: 0.5-4.1°C
  • Exterior Wall Temperature Reduction: 3.8°C
  • Noise Reduction: 9.8 db
  • Air Pollutant Removal (NO2): 18-35% (Street Canyon)
  • Air Pollutant Removal (PM10): 22-50% (Street Canyon)
  • Rainwater Runoff Retained: <75%
  • Water Quality (NO3 Reduction): 30-83%
  • Water Quality (TSS Reduction): 33-99%
  • Water Quality (P Reduction): 15-30%
  • Property Value Uplift: 2.5%

Technical Guidance (UK Context)

  • Structural Assessment: The supporting wall must be assessed by a structural engineer to ensure it can bear the additional load of the living wall system (including saturated weight, vegetation, mounting system, wind load). Weights vary significantly by system type (e.g., modular panels, felt pockets) but can be substantial (50-100+ kg/m²).
  • Wall Condition & Waterproofing: The underlying wall surface must be sound and weather-resistant. While most systems create a ventilation gap, ensure the wall itself prevents moisture ingress into the building fabric. Some systems may require an additional impermeable membrane fixed to the wall behind the living wall structure.
  • System Selection: Choose a system appropriate for the scale, budget, location (sun/shade), and desired maintenance level. Common types include modular panel systems (pre-planted or planted in situ), felt pocket systems, or container systems fixed to a frame. Consider material durability, fire resistance, and manufacturer warranties.
  • Mounting & Framework: Securely fix the mounting frame or rails to the structural wall according to manufacturer specifications and engineering calculations. Ensure appropriate fixings are used for the wall substrate. A ventilation gap between the system and the wall is usually required.
  • Irrigation & Drainage: An automated irrigation system (typically drip irrigation integrated into the panels/modules) is ESSENTIAL for most UK living walls. Requires a reliable water supply (mains water often needed), pressure regulation, filtration, control unit (timer), and potentially nutrient dosing (fertigation). Drainage must be managed: collect runoff at the base (e.g., into a gutter or planter connected to drainage) to prevent staining or waterlogging below.
  • Growing Medium: Use lightweight, stable, free-draining growing media specifically designed for vertical systems. Avoid standard soil. Media may be mineral-based (e.g., rockwool in some systems) or lightweight organic mixes.
  • Plant Selection: Choose species suitable for vertical growth, the specific light conditions (sun/shade/wind exposure), and the UK climate. Consider evergreen vs. deciduous, flowering period, texture, and drought/cold tolerance. UK native species like Hart's Tongue Fern (Asplenium scolopendrium), Ivy (Hedera helix - use cautiously), Thyme (Thymus polytrichus), Bellflower (Campanula spp.), or Sedums can be incorporated, alongside suitable non-native perennials and grasses. Planting density needs to ensure good coverage.
  • Installation: Follow manufacturer's instructions meticulously for framework, irrigation setup, module/panel installation, planting, and commissioning the irrigation system.
  • Maintenance: Requires REGULAR maintenance: Check irrigation system function (emitters, filters, timer), prune/trim plants, replace failed plants, manage pests/diseases, replenish nutrients (via fertigation or slow-release fertiliser), and check structural fixings. Maintenance frequency depends on the system and planting but is typically several times per year.

Products Needed

  • Living Wall System (Modules/Panels/Pockets & Frame)
  • Wall Waterproofing Membrane (If required)
  • Irrigation System Components (Drip lines, emitters, timer, filter, pump, tank if needed)
  • Fertigation Unit (Optional)
  • Vertical Growing Medium
  • Plants (Suitable species, often supplied as plugs)
  • Base Drainage Channel / Guttering
  • Structural Fixings (Wall specific)
